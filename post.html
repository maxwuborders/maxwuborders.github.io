Costa Rica - an unlikely borderland

Introduction to the project

Posted by Max Wu on May 16, 2022

Costa Rica. A small country in Central America known for its lush rainforests, pristine beaches, and relaxed people. Despite only having a population of 5 million, it is widely thought to be one of the “happiest countries on Earth”. However, while in the eyes of outsiders, Costa Rica might be akin to paradise, this cannot be farther from the truth for border dwellers.

Over the next few blog posts, I will document the stories of three very different people, all living in Costa Rica, who interact with borders on a daily basis. Over the past few days, I have interviewed a call center worker, a Nicaraguan housekeeper, and a Bribri tribesman in order to understand their unique situations as border dwellers and give them a platform to amplify their voices. I am sure that while their experiences with borders might vary by a great margin, many common themes will arise. Additionally, the interviews were carried out in Spanish and I did my best to faithfully translate the source material to English.

This blog project was inspired by the COML3336 - Border Environments class at Cornell. In particular, I was inspired by our discussions on Rivera’s Sleep Dealer and digital borders, LaPensee’s Thunderbird Strike and discrimination, as well as Press’s Dirty Work and the concept of immigrants performing unddesired jobs.
